Tungsten is a general-purpose, multi-paradigm programming language designed and implemented by Erik Peterson.

Blocks are indentation-delimited, units of measurement are checked at compile time, and the language ships with an unusually rich set of literals.

It features a self-hosted compiler, plus interpreters written in [[Ruby]], [[C]] (a bytecode VM), and [[Tungsten]] itself. Code can lower straight to the GPU (Metal today, with CUDA coming soon). An autotuner searches for the fastest kernel to maximize performance.

The result is a language with the expressiveness of [[Ruby]] and the speed of [[C]], thanks to [[LLVM]].

Primary sources of inspiration are [[Ruby]], [[C]], [[Python]], and [[JavaScript]].

Known bug: you will not always agree with Tungsten.
