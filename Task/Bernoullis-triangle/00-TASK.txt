Bernoulli's triangle is an arithmetic and geometric figure quite similar to the closely related Pascal's triangle.

Both start with a single value of 1 in the first row, (row 0). Rather than every new row getting a 1 added to the left and right ends of the row, every new row <math>n</math> gets a 1 added to the left and <math>2^n</math> added to the right.

Its first few rows look like this: <b>
     1
    1 2
   1 3 4
  1 4 7 8  </b>
where each element of each row is either 1, <math>2^n</math>,  or the sum of the two elements right above it.

Bernoulli's triangle may also be generated using binomials. For any non-negative integer <math>n</math> and for any integer <math>k</math> included between '''0''' and '''n''', the component in row <math>n</math> and column <math>k</math> is given by: 

<math> \sum_{p=0}^k {n \choose p} </math>


Many different integer sequences appear in Bernoulli's triangle.
