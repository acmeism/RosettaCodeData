=Experimental Verification of the NKTg Law in Earth Orbit Based on NASA’s 2025 Earth Dataset=

You are encouraged to solve this task according to the task description, using any language you may know.

==1. Summary==

The NKTg Law describes the variation of an object’s inertia using the function:

NKTg = f(x, v, m)

Where:

x: position  
v: velocity  
m: mass  

The two central quantities of the law are:

NKTg₁ = x × p (position–momentum interaction)

NKTg₂ = (dm/dt) × p (mass variation–momentum interaction)

Where p = m × v, and dm/dt is the rate of mass loss over time.

This study publishes the experimental verification of the NKTg Law using Earth orbital data obtained from the NASA JPL Horizons system and simulates the planetary motion parameters for 2025 assuming small atmospheric mass loss.

==2. Research Objectives==

Verify the predictive ability of the NKTg Law on planetary motion.

Analyze Earth’s orbital position, velocity, and mass variation using the NKTg model.

Compare simulation results with NASA’s observed data.

==4. Conclusion==

Orbital stability: The quantity NKTg₁ of Earth maintains a target constant, helping the planet self-adjust its velocity when the distance to the Sun changes.

NKTg₂ effect: With a small atmospheric mass loss rate, the value of NKTg₂ becomes negative, acting as a resistance mechanism that stabilizes the system.

== Experimental Verification of the NKTg Law in Earth Orbit Based on NASA’s 2025 Earth Dataset ==

Given Earth orbital data from 2024:

x (position, m)  
v (velocity, m/s)  
m (mass, kg)  

dm/dt = −1.8 kg/s

Compute for each date:

1. Linear momentum

      p = m·v

2. NKTg₁

      NKTg₁ = x·p

3. NKTg₂

      NKTg₂ = (dm/dt)·p

4. Compare simulated velocity with NASA observed velocity.

5. Compute relative error (%).

Programs should output calculated values and relative error for each 2025 date.
