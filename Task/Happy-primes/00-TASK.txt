Happy primes are happy numbers that are prime. A happy number is one where repeatedly taking the sum of the squares of the digits of an integer ends up at 1. 

7 is a happy number: 7 49 97 130 10 1


An unhappy number is one that never reaches a stable value, rather it resolves to a repeating cycle.

21 is an unhappy number: 21 5 25 29 85 89 145 42 20 4 16 37 58 89 145 ...


Happy primes are common in lower numbers. At several points they make up more than half of all happy numbers. They get much less common as integers get larger.
